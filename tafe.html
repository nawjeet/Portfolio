An honourable moment.

Graduation ceremony, also known as convocation, invocation or commencement, occurs when students become graduates after successfully completing their studies in either diploma of academic degree.

Start of Ceremony

Normally, graduation ceremonies last about 90-150 minutes. Ceremonial staffs are expected to do their best to ensure the ceremony is smooth and organised in a timely manner.

Curtin University organises two graduation ceremonies yearly; one for Diploma and one for Degree and Masters.

Academic Procession

When the ceremony has started around 15-20 minutes, the academic procession starts where all the university staff along with board members enter the ceremony. Graduands must remain standing until the entire academic procession and Chancellor have taken seats.

Keynote Speaker

Once all graduates are seated to their respective places, an inspirational and motivational speech is addressed to the future endeavours. The duration of the speech is usually 10-15 minutes.

Welcome Address

A brief welcome is addressed to all faculty, graduates and invitees by the Vice-Chancellor or President of the academic board.

Certificate & Award

All graduates are usually made seated in an alphabetical order and are presented with their certificates of completion following with the presentation of awards to deserving and hard-working students.

Walking the Stage

This is the most important part of the ceremony. Graduates will walk up the stage one by one and will feel honourable and will feel the claps of everyone to cheer up your achievement.

Farewell Address

A representative student, usually the ambassador, delivers a speech thanking everyone who attended the ceremony and gives a valedictory address to friends and family.

Academic & Graduates Exit

Once the speech is over, the academic procession takes place where everyone stands up for the departure of the academic staff. The graduates are then greeted and followed by friends and family.
